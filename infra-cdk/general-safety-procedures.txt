1. PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT (PPE) REQUIREMENTS
=============================================================================
All personnel entering Unit B must comply with the following minimum PPE:

Zone A — General floor access:
  - Hard hat (EN 397)
  - Safety boots (steel toe-cap, EN ISO 20345 S3)
  - High-visibility vest (Class 2)
  - Safety glasses (EN 166)

Zone B — Rotating machinery area (includes Pump-01, Compressor-01, Motor-01):
  - All Zone A PPE, PLUS:
  - Hearing protection (min 25 dB SNR) when any machine is running
  - Gloves (cut level C minimum) when handling mechanical components

Zone C — Conveyor and materials handling area (Conveyor-01):
  - All Zone A PPE, PLUS:
  - No loose clothing, ties, or jewellery
  - Long sleeves are mandatory
  - Gloves prohibited when approaching moving conveyor belt (entanglement risk)

Zone D — High-pressure systems (Turbine-01, Compressor-01 pressure side):
  - All Zone A PPE, PLUS:
  - Face shield (full-face, EN 166) when breaking any pressurised joints
  - Chemical-resistant gloves when handling hydraulic oil or heat transfer fluid

2. EMERGENCY SHUTDOWN SEQUENCE (SITE-WIDE)
=============================================================================
In the event of fire, major leak, electrical fault, or injury:

  STEP 1 — Alert: Sound the site alarm (pull red handle at any fire point or
           press ESTOP on the main control panel in the control room).

  STEP 2 — Evacuate: All non-essential personnel evacuate via marked green
           exit routes to Assembly Point A (north car park, Gate 3).

  STEP 3 — Isolate: The Control Room Operator (CRO) executes the following
           in order:
             a) ESTOP all running machinery via the DCS Safety Shutdown panel.
             b) Close the main gas isolation valve (red handle, gas room).
             c) Activate the firewater deluge system if fire is confirmed.
             d) Trip the 11 kV incomer if electrical fault is involved.

  STEP 4 — Report: CRO calls the Emergency Services (dial 999 from landline)
           and the Site Emergency Coordinator (SEC) simultaneously.

  STEP 5 — Do not re-enter the building until the all-clear is given by the
           SEC or emergency services.

4. INCIDENT REPORTING PROCEDURE
=============================================================================
All incidents — including near-misses — MUST be reported. Failure to report
is a disciplinary offence.

  IMMEDIATE (within 15 minutes of incident):
    - Notify the Shift Supervisor verbally.
    - If injury: call First Aider (Ext. 2400) and, if serious, dial 999.
    - Preserve the scene — do not move equipment or materials until authorised
      by HSE Manager unless doing so is necessary to prevent further injury.

  SHORT-TERM (within 1 hour):
    - Complete an Initial Incident Notification form (paper copies at each
      station board; electronic form at https://intranet.acme.local/hse).
    - Photograph the scene from at least four angles before any clean-up.

  FULL REPORT (within 24 hours):
    - Complete the full Incident Investigation Report (IIR) in the HSE system.
    - Include: root cause, contributing factors, immediate corrective actions,
      and recommendations to prevent recurrence.
    - Reportable injuries (RIDDOR) are notified to the HSE by the HSE Manager.

5. LOCKOUT / TAGOUT (LOTO) — SOP-LOTO-002 SUMMARY
=============================================================================
LOTO must be applied before any maintenance work on machinery. No exceptions.

  Step 1 — Notify: Inform the Shift Supervisor that the equipment will be
           isolated. Record it in the Permit to Work (PTW) log.

  Step 2 — Identify all energy sources: electrical (main isolator, VFD,
           controls), pneumatic (instrument air supply), hydraulic
           (accumulator), gravity (suspended loads), thermal (hot surfaces).

  Step 3 — Isolate: Switch off and physically lock the main isolator with a
           personal padlock. Each worker applies their own lock. All locks must
           be on before work starts.

  Step 4 — Tag: Attach a red DANGER tag to each isolator with the worker's
           name, date, and contact number.

  Step 5 — Verify zero energy:
             - Electrical: test with approved voltage tester (CAT III min).
             - Pneumatic: open downstream bleed valve; confirm zero pressure.
             - Stored energy: discharge any capacitors; block suspended loads.

  Step 6 — Work safely. Do not remove another person's lock under any
           circumstances. If a key is lost, the HSE Manager must authorise
           removal using the master key procedure.

  Step 7 — Restore: Remove all tools and materials. Replace all guards.
           Each worker removes their own lock in reverse order. Confirm with
           Shift Supervisor before re-energising. Update the PTW log.

6. PERMIT TO WORK (PTW) SYSTEM
=============================================================================
A PTW is mandatory for:
  - Any maintenance inside a machine guard or enclosure
  - Hot work (welding, grinding, cutting) anywhere on site
  - Working at height (> 2 m above floor level)
  - Confined space entry
  - Any work that requires breaking a pressurised or hazardous system

PTW forms are issued by the Shift Supervisor and must be signed by:
  - Issuing authority (Shift Supervisor or Plant Manager)
  - Person(s) performing the work
  - Safety observer (if required by risk assessment)

PTW expires at the end of the shift. A new PTW must be issued for each shift.
All completed PTWs are retained for 3 years in the HSE archive.
