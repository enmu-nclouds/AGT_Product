MACHINE MANUAL — COMPRESSOR-01 (Reciprocating Air Compressor)
Version: 2.7  |  Site: Acme Manufacturing Plant, Unit B  |  Date: 2024-01

=============================================================================
1. MACHINE OVERVIEW
=============================================================================
Compressor-01 is a two-stage reciprocating air compressor supplying instrument
air to the pneumatic control system at up to 8.5 bar gauge. Drive motor: 55 kW,
3-phase, 1480 RPM, direct-coupled via flexible coupling. Delivery capacity:
35 m³/min FAD at 7 bar. Interstage cooling via an aftercooler and moisture
separator.

=============================================================================
2. NORMAL OPERATING RANGES
=============================================================================
  Cylinder head temperature (stage 1):  80–110°C
  Cylinder head temperature (stage 2):  90–120°C
  Vibration (frame, RMS):               0.8–2.8 mm/s
  Discharge pressure (instrument air):  7.0–8.5 bar
  Interstage pressure:                  2.8–3.4 bar
  RPM:                                  1450–1510
  Lube oil pressure:                    2.0–3.5 bar
  Lube oil temperature:                 65–85°C

Warning thresholds:
  Any cylinder head temp > 130°C — check valve condition and intercooler flow
  Vibration > 4.0 mm/s — inspect valve plates and piston rings
  Lube oil pressure < 1.5 bar — shut down; risk of bearing damage
  Discharge pressure > 9.0 bar — safety relief valve should lift; investigate

Critical (shut down immediately):
  Cylinder head temp > 150°C
  Vibration > 6.5 mm/s
  Lube oil pressure < 1.0 bar
  Any knocking or metallic impact noise from cylinders

=============================================================================
3. MAINTENANCE SCHEDULE
=============================================================================
Weekly:
  - Check and drain moisture separator and aftercooler drain traps
  - Record cylinder head temps, frame vibration, and lube oil pressure
  - Inspect V-belt tension (deflection < 15 mm under 10 N load) — if belt-driven
  - Verify auto-drain solenoids are cycling (check timer light on panel)

Monthly:
  - Change lube oil filter element (P/N: LF-9011-OEM)
  - Inspect suction and discharge valve plates; replace if pitting visible
  - Check piston rod packing for excessive blow-by (max 2% leakage rate)
  - Calibrate unloader solenoids and verify no-load start cycle

Annual:
  - Full valve overhaul — replace all valve plates and seats
    (Stage 1: P/N VK-S1-C01 / Stage 2: P/N VK-S2-C01)
  - Replace piston rings and rider rings (P/N: PR-63MM-PTFE)
  - Drain, flush, and refill crankcase with ISO VG100 mineral oil (12 L)
  - Hydrostatic test of interstage piping at 1.5× design pressure
  - Motor megger test (min 50 MΩ at 1000 V DC)
  - Replace coupling flexible element (P/N: FE-OMEGA-10)

=============================================================================
4. TROUBLESHOOTING
=============================================================================

4.1 High Cylinder Head Temperature (> 130°C)
  Likely causes:
    a) Fouled or failed discharge valve — valve not opening fully, gas re-expansion
    b) Blocked intercooler — scale buildup reducing heat transfer
    c) High ambient temperature combined with high load
    d) Incorrect valve clearance (re-seat gap out of spec)
  Steps:
    1. Reduce load to 60% via unloader solenoid and monitor for 5 minutes.
    2. Check intercooler coolant flow (should be > 8 L/min).
    3. If temp does not drop to < 110°C within 10 minutes, shut down.
    4. Remove and inspect cylinder head valve plate; replace if worn
       (Stage 1 kit: P/N VK-S1-C01).

4.2 High Vibration (> 4.0 mm/s)
  Likely causes:
    a) Loose flywheel or coupling bolts
    b) Worn piston rod bearing (small-end bush)
    c) Cracked or broken valve plate causing asymmetric loading
    d) Foundation bolt loosening
  Steps:
    1. Perform a bolted-joint check on all frame and foundation bolts (45 Nm).
    2. Listen for regular knocking (= broken valve) or irregular knock (= bearing).
    3. Shut down and inspect crankcase for metal debris via sight glass.
    4. If debris found, do not restart; engage maintenance team for major overhaul.

4.3 Low Discharge Pressure (< 7.0 bar) — System Not Meeting Demand
  Likely causes:
    a) Excessive demand (other system leak)
    b) Worn piston rings — reduced volumetric efficiency
    c) Leaking discharge valve
  Steps:
    1. Check for air leaks on the distribution header; listen with ultrasonic detector.
    2. Perform a simple volumetric efficiency test: close the receiver outlet,
       time how long it takes to build from 0 to 8 bar (should be < 4 minutes).
    3. If efficiency is low, replace piston rings at next planned outage.

6. SHUTDOWN PROCEDURE
=============================================================================
Controlled shutdown:
  1. Energise the unloader solenoid to place the compressor on no-load.
  2. Run on no-load for 3 minutes to cool cylinder heads.
  3. Stop motor via main isolator or MCC contactor.
  4. Close the receiver outlet isolating valve.
  5. Vent receiver pressure to zero via drain valve before any maintenance.
  6. Lock out / tag out per SOP-LOTO-002.

Emergency stop:
  1. Hit the ESTOP pushbutton at the local panel.
  2. Inform the control room immediately.
  3. Do NOT vent or open any pressurised component until system is fully at rest
     and pressure is confirmed zero on local gauge.
  4. Incident log must be completed within 1 hour (see general-safety-procedures).
